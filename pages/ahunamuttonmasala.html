Traditional Mutton Masala Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 250g Mutton
  • 1 Sachet Ahuna Mutton Masala
  • 200g Onion
  • Mustard Oil
  • 3 Whole Garlic Buds
  • Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • Salt

Instructions:

  1. Step 1: Mix 250g mutton with 1 sachet of Purvaiya Ahuna Mutton Masala, 200g sliced onion, ginger garlic paste, mustard oil, 2 whole garlic buds and salt.
  2. Step 2: Add the marinated mutton to a pot. Cover tightly and cook on low heat for 40–50 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Step 3: Cook until the meat is tender and the gravy is rich. Serve hot with roti or rice.

The Story Of Ahuna Mutton

Ahuna Mutton is a traditional delicacy from the Mithila region of Bihar, known for its slow-cooking technique and rich, earthy flavors. The word "Ahuna" comes from the local method of cooking meat in a sealed pot over a low flame, allowing the spices and juices to blend deeply without losing aroma.

This dish traces its roots to rural households where clay pots were used to prepare meals with patience and care, often during special occasions and family gatherings. The slow cooking process ensured that the meat absorbed every bit of flavor, making it uniquely tender and aromatic.

Over time, Ahuna Mutton became a symbol of authentic Mithila cuisine, celebrated for its bold taste, minimal use of water, and reliance on natural flavors released during cooking. Today, it continues to carry the legacy of tradition, offering a nostalgic and soulful experience with every bite.
